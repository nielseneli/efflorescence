Mechanical

Last sprint our flower had a bad habit of grabbing people with its oversize petals, so we scaled the flower and the attached petals way down. Now they’re still big enough to stand out but they won’t take anyone captive. We laser cut them this time instead of 3D printing them because the process was way faster and the organic wood look matches our aesthetic more than brightly colored plastic. We also wanted to achieve our MVP of having 3 flowers, so we made two more. To make the flower more eye catching, we added color, specifically in making the petals purple and making the center of the flower a yellow orange gradient. Finally, we started on the arch for the flowers to rest on. We made the two walls of the arch and mounted the flowers on those.

Software

A switch statement moves the bloom and turn servos following the input from the three sensors. It’s pretty twitchy and we’re definitely looking forward to integrating the other two sensors to increase the resolution. Additionally, the bit where the heart of the code is a switch statement that switches on the value of a variable that is decided by kind of approximating bitshifting---that bit feels pretty jank.
